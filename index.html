Mikael Svensson

Health Economist & Professor

I am a health economist and professor at the School of Public Health and Community Medicine at the University of Gothenburg in Sweden. My research focuses on economic evaluation in healthcare and public health, and health technology assessment.

Email Google Scholar LinkedIn CV

Research Interests

Economic Evaluation

Theory and applications of economic evaluation methods in healthcare and public health interventions, including cost-effectiveness and cost-benefit analysis.

Health Technology Assessment

Systematic evaluation of the properties and effects of health technologies, including medical devices, diagnostic tools, and pharmaceutical interventions.

Healthcare Policy

Evidence-based approaches to healthcare policy development and implementation, with focus on resource allocation and priority setting.

Current Research Projects

Reproducible Cost-Effectiveness Models

Can we trust health economic evaluations of new pharmaceuticals? This Forte-funded project examines the reproducibility of pharmacoeconomic models and investigates the impact of researcher degrees of freedom in cost-effectiveness analyses of cancer drugs.

Learn more →

The Evidence-Base and Value of Cancer Drugs

Evaluating the clinical and cost-effectiveness evidence used in reimbursement decisions for new cancer drugs in Sweden. This project examines how surrogate endpoints and limited trial data impact drug pricing and healthcare costs.
